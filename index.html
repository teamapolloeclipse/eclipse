A solar eclipse happens when the Moon passes between the Sun and Earth

A lunar eclipse happens when the Earth comes between the Sun and the Moon, casting a reddish hue on the Moon

Study

Missions Done by NASA to study Eclipse

Hinode, launched in 2006, studies the Sun's magnetic fields and their impact on solar activity, offering insights into solar eclipses.



LRO, orbiting the Moon, indirectly aids our understanding of lunar phenomena, despite not studying lunar eclipses.



Launched in 1995, SOHO, a NASA-ESA collaboration at the L1 Lagrange point, continuously observes the Sun, supplying essential data, including during eclipses.



NASA's Artemis program aims to return humans to the Moon, with data collected during these missions potentially aiding lunar eclipse studies.

Not So Known Facts

How can I safely view the Sun at any time?

Indirect methods like pinhole projectors and solar viewing glasses are safe for observing the Sun, while magnification tools require a properly fitted solar filter for safety.

How long does a total solar eclipse last?

Total solar eclipses last anywhere from 10 seconds to about 7.5 minutes

The shortest total solar eclipse happened on Feb. 3, 919 CE, and lasted just 9 seconds.

the longest total solar eclipse will occur on July 16, 2186, and will last 7 minutes 29 seconds. Its path will sweep across Colombia, Venezuela, and Guyana

Why don't eclipses occur every new moon?

The Moon's orbit is "tilted" relative to the Sun-Earth line. As a result, sometimes the Moon's shadow is too high above Earth and sometimes it is too low. Other times, it is just right

What is a sunspot?

Occasionally, dark spots freckle the face of the Sun. These are sunspots, cooler regions on the Sun's visible surface caused by a concentration of magnetic field line
